THEMATIC RELATIVE VALUE (PAIRS) ENGINE
VERSION 1.0

---

OBJECTIVE

The Thematic Relative Value Engine is designed to systematically identify and execute high-conviction spread trades between two related assets. The goal is to generate consistent, short-duration returns by capturing relative outperformance driven by macro regimes, sector rotation, and narrative shifts.

This engine does not rely on outright market direction. It isolates edge through divergence, convergence, and trend expansion between paired instruments.

---

CORE FUNCTION

The engine continuously monitors a fixed library of high-quality pairs and evaluates:

1. Relative price movement (ratio-based analysis)
2. Statistical deviation (z-score vs rolling mean)
3. Momentum and trend alignment
4. Macro and narrative context (theme validation)

Trades are only generated when both:
- A measurable spread signal exists
- A valid macro or narrative context supports the move

---

SIGNAL CONSTRUCTION

Each pair is evaluated using a standardized framework:

1. RATIO CALCULATION
   ratio = price(long) / price(short)

2. STATISTICAL POSITIONING
   - Rolling mean (20–60 day window)
   - Standard deviation
   - Z-score of current ratio

3. MOMENTUM LAYER
   - 5-day ROC
   - 10-day ROC
   - Short-term directional alignment

4. TREND CONTEXT
   - Position vs moving averages
   - Breakout vs mean-reverting structure

---

TRADE MODES

Each pair can operate in one of two modes:

MEAN REVERSION MODE
- Triggered when spread reaches statistical extremes
- Expectation: reversion toward historical mean
- Typically shorter duration (2–5 days)

MOMENTUM MODE
- Triggered when spread breaks trend with confirmation
- Expectation: continuation of divergence
- Typically medium duration (3–10 days)

---

THEME ENGINE (CRITICAL LAYER)

Narrative and macro context must validate the signal.

Themes are derived from:
- News flow (Benzinga + EODHD)
- Macro conditions (rates, oil, volatility)
- LLM classification (OpenAI)

Examples:
- "AI expansion"
- "AI deceleration"
- "Rates rising"
- "Risk-off"
- "Liquidity expansion"
- "Energy shock"

RULE:
Narrative alone does not trigger trades.
Narrative enables trades when signal confirms.

---

CONFIDENCE SCORING

Each trade is assigned a confidence score (0–100) based on:

- Z-score extremity
- Momentum alignment
- Trend confirmation
- Theme alignment
- Volatility regime (optional ORATS overlay)

Only trades above a defined threshold are eligible for execution.

---

OUTPUT STRUCTURE

For each qualified pair:

- Pair ID
- Long Asset
- Short Asset
- Mode (Mean Reversion / Momentum)
- Confidence Score
- Suggested Risk Allocation (in Risk Units)
- Expected Holding Period (days)
- Supporting Theme Tag(s)

---

RISK FRAMEWORK

- Engine operates within Opportunistic Allocation Bucket
- Target allocation: 15–25% of total portfolio
- Max concurrent positions: 3–5 pairs
- Standard trade size: 0.5–1.5 Risk Units

Avoid stacking highly correlated pairs simultaneously.
