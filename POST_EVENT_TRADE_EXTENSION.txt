POST-EVENT TRADE EXTENSION ENGINE
VERSION 2.0 (EXTENSION-ONLY MODEL)

---

OBJECTIVE

The Post-Event Trade Extension Engine is designed to extract additional directional edge from trades that have already been initiated and are working as expected.

This engine does not scan for new opportunities. It activates only after an existing event-driven trade (primarily short iron condors around earnings or major catalysts) has reached the post-event phase.

Its purpose is to determine whether a second, short-duration directional trade should be deployed based on realized price action, volatility collapse, and contextual alignment.

---

CORE PHILOSOPHY

The engine operates on a simple principle:

We only deploy additional capital when:
- We already have a profitable or controlled trade
- The market has revealed new information
- A measurable post-event edge exists

This ensures:
- Higher signal quality
- Reduced noise vs scanning systems
- Increased capital efficiency per trade
- Preservation of system discipline

---

ACTIVATION CONDITIONS (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

The engine only activates when ALL conditions are met:

1. An existing trade is active or recently closed
   - Typically short iron condor around earnings

2. The underlying event has occurred
   - Earnings release or major catalyst

3. The original trade outcome is:
   - Profitable OR
   - Within expected loss tolerance (no breakdown scenario)

4. Post-event price data is available
   - Gap, open structure, and initial reaction

If these conditions are not met, the engine does not run.

---

POST-EVENT SNAPSHOT (STATE CAPTURE)

At the time of evaluation (typically open or shortly after), capture:

- Actual move vs expected move (EM)
- ATR multiple of move
- Direction of move (up/down)
- Gap structure:
  - Hold
  - Fade
  - Stall
- Volatility compression (IV crush)
- News/event sentiment (LLM classification)

This snapshot defines the decision state for the engine.

---

DISPLACEMENT CLASSIFICATION

Each event is categorized based on:

1. MAGNITUDE
- Move relative to EM
- Move relative to ATR

2. STRUCTURE
- Gap and hold
- Gap and fade
- Gap and consolidation

3. CONTEXT
- Alignment with prior trend
- Alignment with broader market and sector

---

HISTORICAL PATTERN LAYER

For each ticker (or similar class), evaluate historical outcomes under similar conditions:

- When move exceeds X% of EM
- When gap structure matches current state
- When sentiment classification is similar

Output:

- Probability of continuation over 1–5 days
- Probability of mean reversion
- Average magnitude of follow-through or reversal
- Confidence band based on sample size

This layer becomes more accurate over time as data accumulates.

---

REGIME OVERLAY

Apply macro and market context filters:

- Dealer gamma / volatility regime (from existing system layer)
- Broad market trend (SPX / QQQ direction)
- Sector strength or weakness

This determines directional bias:
- Favor continuation
- Favor mean reversion
- Neutral (no trade)

---

DECISION FRAMEWORK

The engine produces only three possible outcomes:

1. CONTINUE
- Enter directional trade aligned with post-event move
- Used when continuation probability and context align

2. FADE
- Enter directional trade against the move
- Used when overextension and reversal conditions align

3. PASS
- No trade
- Default outcome when edge is not clear

PASS must remain the most common outcome to preserve edge quality.

---

TRADE PROFILE

- Holding period: 1–5 days
- Risk allocation: 0.5–1.5 Risk Units
- Instruments:
  - Shares (preferred for simplicity)
  - Defined-risk options (optional)

No complex structures or intraday dependency.

---

CONFIDENCE SCORING

Each potential extension trade is assigned a score (0–100) based on:

- Displacement magnitude (EM and ATR)
- Gap structure quality
- Momentum or exhaustion characteristics
- Historical pattern alignment
- Event clarity (earnings/news sentiment)
- Regime alignment

Only trades above a defined threshold are eligible.

---

OUTPUT STRUCTURE

For each evaluated ticker:

- Ticker
- Decision: CONTINUE / FADE / PASS
- Direction (Long / Short if applicable)
- Confidence Score
- Suggested Risk Allocation (Risk Units)
- Expected Holding Period (days)
- Entry Preference (open, pullback, confirmation)
- Event Tag (earnings, volatility shock, etc.)

---

RISK FRAMEWORK

- Operates within Opportunistic Allocation Bucket
- Typical allocation: 10–20% of portfolio
- Position size per trade: 0.5–1.5 Risk Units
- Avoid stacking correlated exposures across multiple tickers

---

SYSTEM CONSTRAINTS

- No standalone scanning for new trades
- Activation only from existing positions
- End-of-day driven with optional first-hour confirmation
- Deterministic outputs
- Finite and controlled signal set

---

EXPLORATION AREAS (FUTURE ITERATION)

---

SUMMARY

This engine transforms completed or successful event-driven trades into a second-stage decision framework.

It leverages:
- realized price action
- volatility collapse
- contextual and historical patterns

to determine whether additional directional capital should be deployed.

The result is a disciplined system that increases return per trade without increasing noise, frequency, or complexity.
