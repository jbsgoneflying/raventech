Tier Filter
Pair tier classification

The 20-pair universe is organized into three tiers:

  • Tier 1 — Core Index / Style: QQQ/IWM, SPY/IWM, QQQ/SPY, RSP/SPY, SPHB/SPLV, SPY/EFA. Large-cap style and size rotation.
  • Tier 2 — Sector Rotation: XLY/XLP, XLF/XLK, XLE/SPY, XLI/XLU, XLB/XLI, SOXX/IGV, XBI/XLV, XHB/SPY. Sector relative strength.
  • Tier 3 — Macro / Cross-Asset: GLD/TLT, USO/SPY, UUP/SPY, IBIT/QQQ, VIXY/SPY, HYG/TLT. Cross-asset regime signals.

Desk view: Tier 1 pairs are the most liquid and carry the tightest spreads. Tier 3 pairs offer the most diversification but require macro conviction.
Mode Filter
Trade mode classification

Each pair signal operates in one of two modes:

  • Mean Reversion: Spread has reached a statistical extreme (high z-score). Expectation is reversion toward the rolling mean. Typically 2–5 day holding period.
  • Momentum: Spread has broken trend with confirmation. Expectation is continuation of divergence. Typically 3–10 day holding period.

Desk view: Mean-reversion trades work best in range-bound regimes with positive gamma. Momentum trades favour trending regimes with strong narrative support.
Min Score
Minimum confidence score

Composite confidence score (0–100) combining:

  • Z-score extremity: How far the spread has deviated from its rolling mean
  • Momentum alignment: 5-day and 10-day ROC confirmation
  • Trend confirmation: Position vs moving averages, breakout vs reversion structure
  • Theme alignment: Macro/narrative context support from headlines and LLM classification
  • Volatility regime: ORATS IV overlay (when enabled)

Desk view: 75+ is A+ (high conviction, multiple confirmations). 50–74 is standard. Below 50 is watchlist only — not tradeable without additional confluence.

Active Themes
Theme Engine (Critical Layer)

Themes are derived from recent headlines (Benzinga + EODHD) and classified using deterministic rules + LLM annotation.

Key rule: Narrative alone does NOT trigger trades. Narrative enables trades when the statistical signal confirms.

  • AI Expansion / Deceleration: Drives SOXX/IGV, QQQ/SPY, IBIT/QQQ
  • Rates Rising / Falling: Drives GLD/TLT, XLF/XLK, HYG/TLT
  • Risk-Off: Drives VIXY/SPY, XLY/XLP, SPY/EFA
  • Energy Shock: Drives XLE/SPY, USO/SPY
  • Liquidity Expansion: Drives SPHB/SPLV, SPY/IWM, IBIT/QQQ

Desk view: Read the theme tags on each pair to understand WHY it's eligible. If you disagree with the theme classification, treat the signal with caution — your macro conviction matters.

A+ Pair Signals

Desk view: These are your primary candidates. Check the suggested risk allocation (0.5–1.5 RU) and expected holding period. Max 3–5 concurrent pairs to avoid correlation stacking.

How to Trade Relative Value Pairs

Entry Rules
  • Mean Reversion: Enter when z-score exceeds threshold with theme support
  • Momentum: Enter on trend break with directional ROC confirmation
  • Always verify the active theme aligns with your macro view
Risk Allocation
  • Target: 15–25% of portfolio (opportunistic bucket)
  • Standard trade: 0.5–1.5 Risk Units per pair
  • Max concurrent: 3–5 pairs
Holding Period
  • Mean Reversion: 2–5 days
  • Momentum: 3–10 days
  • Exit on z-score mean crossover or theme invalidation
Correlation Guard
  • Avoid stacking highly correlated pairs
  • Overlap filter flags correlated signals automatically
  • Diversify across tiers for uncorrelated exposure
