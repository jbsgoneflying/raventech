Market Gamma Context
Market Gamma Context (SPX Dealer Positioning)

What is dealer gamma? Options market makers (dealers) must hedge their positions. When they're "long gamma," they buy dips and sell rips. When "short gamma," they do the opposite.

Why it matters for Red Dog:

  • Positive (Long) Gamma: Dealers are your ally. Their hedging naturally supports mean reversion — they buy weakness and sell strength, creating "cushions" at extremes.
  • Negative (Short) Gamma: Dealers work against you. Their hedging amplifies moves — they sell weakness and buy strength, making breakdowns more likely to accelerate rather than reverse.

How to use it:

  • Positive gamma: Trade Red Dog setups with normal position sizing. The environment supports reversals.
  • Negative gamma: Be selective — only take A+ setups, use smaller size, or require additional confluence (S/R, volume climax).

SPX Trend Filter (21 EMA)
SPX 21 EMA Trend Filter

Why the 21 EMA? Scott Redler (who popularized Red Dog) uses the 8/21 EMA as trend filters. The 21 EMA captures the intermediate-term trend direction.

The Rule:

  • SPX above 21 EMA: Favor BULLISH Red Dog setups (failed breakdowns). These trade WITH the uptrend.
  • SPX below 21 EMA: Favor BEARISH Red Dog setups (failed breakouts). These trade WITH the downtrend.

Counter-trend trades (bullish in downtrend, bearish in uptrend) CAN work, but require higher conviction — A+ scores, tight stops, and additional confluence.

A+ Watchlist
A+ Watchlist

High-quality Red Dog Reversal setups (score >= 75) with multiple confirmation factors:

  • RSI extreme: Oversold (<30) or overbought (>70)
  • Stochastics extreme: <20 or >80
  • SMA20 deviation: Price >9% from 20-day average
  • Volume surge: >1.5x average volume
  • Strong close: Close in favorable zone (>70% or <30% of range)

How to Trade Red Dog Reversals

Entry Rules
  • Bullish: Buy stop above reversal day's high
  • Bearish: Sell stop below reversal day's low
  • Confirm open is within prior day's range
Stop Rules
  • Bullish: Stop at Low B (reversal day low)
  • Bearish: Stop at High B (reversal day high)
  • Add buffer: ATR(14) × 0.25 or $0.10
Targets
  • T1: 1R (risk-reward 1:1)
  • T2: 2R (risk-reward 1:2)
  • T3: SMA20 mean reversion
Position Sizing
  • Scale position to 1-2% account risk
  • Take 50% off at T1
  • Trail remainder with prior bar stop
