Earnings Hold Risk
Earnings Hold Risk

Close-based breach probabilities for earnings day and next day (not open-gap). Quantifies holding through earnings risk.

  • Unconditional: all events — breach rate if you hold to close.
  • Flat open: events where gap was ≤0.25× EM — helps isolate intraday drift risk.
  • Drift: post-gap continuation (EO→EC, EC→NC) — shows how much the move extends after open.

Desk view: use this alongside open-gap stats to decide hold vs exit strategies.

Regime overlay (risk climate)

  • Inputs: SPY realized vol percentile, ticker IV30 percentile, SPY 5D move percentile.
  • Output: tail multiplier (roughly 0.7–2.0×) + a simple trade gate.
  • Desk view: treat this like a “conditions check” — elevated/stress regimes deserve wider wings or no-trade.

Simulated Earnings Risk (MC)
Simulated earnings risk (Monte Carlo)

Empirical simulation of the earnings gap at open (close→open only). No intraday path is modeled.

  • Chance of breach: probability the gap finishes beyond your short strikes.
  • Expected loss / CVaR95: expected and tail intrinsic loss at open (risk-only).
  • Desk view: treat this as “open-gap tail risk” — it’s the risk that blows through short IC wings before you can manage.

Quarter Seasonality
Quarter seasonality

Compares each quarter vs the ticker’s baseline earnings behavior (breach rate + realized/implied ratios).

  • What to look for: quarters with positive breach deltas and high max ratios are typically “wider wings” quarters.
  • Desk view: this is a simple calendar conditioning tool — useful for strike distance and sizing, not a directional signal.
  • Sample size matters: quarters with <3 usable events should be treated as low confidence.

Breach Δ shown in percentage points (pp)
Recommendation uses a heuristic; quarters with <3 usable events are labeled “Avoid (low sample)”.

Earnings Events
Earnings events table

Row-by-row history of implied vs realized earnings gaps and whether each event breached.

  • near-breach: highlighted rows are close to breach (realized/implied ≥ 0.90) — useful for “almost blew out” context.
  • Desk view: scan for clustering — repeated outsized gaps, or a directional tail bias, should influence wing asymmetry and sizing.
