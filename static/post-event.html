Engine 1 IC Outcome

Extension Decision
Post-event extension decision

The engine produces only three possible outcomes:

  • CONTINUE: Enter a directional trade aligned with the post-event move. Continuation probability and macro context both support follow-through.
  • FADE: Enter a directional trade against the move. Overextension and mean-reversion conditions align — the gap is likely to partially reverse.
  • PASS: No trade. Edge is not clear enough. This is the most common and correct outcome — it preserves signal quality.

Desk view: PASS is the default. Only CONTINUE and FADE with high confidence (65+) deserve capital. When in doubt, respect the PASS.

Post-Event Snapshot
Post-event state capture

Captured at the time of evaluation — this snapshot defines the decision state:

  • Actual vs Expected Move: How the realized gap compares to the implied earnings move (EM). Moves within 1x EM are "contained." Above 1.5x EM signals a large displacement.
  • ATR Multiple: Move relative to the 14-day ATR. Context for whether the move is historically extreme for this ticker.
  • Gap Structure: Hold (price sustains), Fade (price reverses), or Stall (price consolidates near open).
  • IV Crush: Magnitude of implied volatility collapse post-event. Larger crush = more premium captured on original trade.

Desk view: Look for gap-and-hold with contained IV crush — that's where follow-through is highest. Gap-and-fade setups are FADE candidates if the reversal is sharp and ATR-excessive.

Displacement Classification
Displacement classification

Each post-event state is classified across three dimensions:

  • Magnitude: Move relative to EM and ATR — small, moderate, large, or extreme
  • Structure: Gap-and-hold, gap-and-fade, or gap-and-consolidation
  • Context: Alignment with prior trend and broader market/sector direction

Desk view: "Large magnitude + gap-and-hold + aligned with prior trend" is the strongest CONTINUE setup. "Extreme magnitude + gap-and-fade + counter-trend" is the strongest FADE setup.

Magnitude
Move magnitude

Classifies the realized move relative to expectations:

  • Small: < 0.5x EM — contained, low follow-through probability
  • Moderate: 0.5–1.0x EM — typical, could go either way
  • Large: 1.0–1.5x EM — significant, higher continuation probability
  • Extreme: > 1.5x EM — potential exhaustion or breakaway
—
Structure
Gap structure

How price behaves after the initial gap:

  • Hold: Price sustains near the gap level — favors continuation
  • Fade: Price reverses toward pre-event close — favors mean reversion
  • Stall: Price consolidates in a narrow range — ambiguous, often a PASS
—
Context Alignment
Trend and market context

Evaluates whether the post-event move aligns with broader conditions:

  • Aligned: Move direction matches prior trend + sector + market. High continuation probability.
  • Counter: Move opposes existing trend. Higher fade probability but also higher risk of breakdown continuation.
  • Neutral: Mixed signals. Typically results in a PASS decision.

Historical Pattern Match
Historical pattern analysis

For this ticker (or similar class), evaluates historical outcomes under similar post-event conditions:

  • Continuation probability: How often the 1–5 day follow-through continued in the gap direction
  • Mean reversion probability: How often the move reversed
  • Average magnitude: Typical follow-through or reversal size
  • Confidence band: Width based on sample size — more data = tighter band

Desk view: Small sample sizes (< 10 events) produce wide confidence bands. Don't over-weight the exact probability — use it as directional guidance alongside the displacement classification.

Trade Profile
Suggested trade parameters

When the decision is CONTINUE or FADE, the engine provides a structured trade profile:

  • Direction: Long or Short
  • Risk allocation: 0.5–1.5 Risk Units (within opportunistic bucket, 10–20% of portfolio)
  • Holding period: 1–5 days — these are short-duration overlay trades
  • Entry preference: Open (immediate), pullback (wait for dip), or confirmation (wait for additional signal)

Desk view: Prefer shares for simplicity. Defined-risk options are acceptable but avoid complex structures. Keep this simple — it's a bolt-on to an existing position, not a standalone strategy.

How to Use Post-Event Extensions

Activation Rules
  • Only activates after an existing trade is active or recently closed
  • The underlying event (earnings/catalyst) must have occurred
  • Original trade must be profitable or within loss tolerance
Risk Framework
  • Operates within the opportunistic allocation bucket
  • Typical allocation: 10–20% of portfolio
  • 0.5–1.5 Risk Units per trade
  • Avoid stacking correlated exposures
Instruments
  • Shares: Preferred for simplicity and flexibility
  • Defined-risk options: Acceptable (debit spreads, long puts/calls)
  • No complex structures or intraday dependency
Key Principle
  • PASS is the most common and correct outcome
  • This engine increases return per trade, not trade frequency
  • Never deploy capital when edge is unclear
